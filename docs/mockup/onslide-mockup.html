Eight decisions — approve or correct each

1

Reserved title zone shipped

Top ~13% stays free for a native PowerPoint title. The chart fills everything below and keeps that frame through every edit.

2

Left rail is a real label column — and rows are objects

Title rows (Phase 1, Build) and task rows (Implementation, QA + polish) are the same thing. A task row's text lives in the rail so its bar stays text-free. Select any row: the app bar offers Add above/below, ↑ ↓ to reorder (try it — the chart follows), indent/outdent, delete. Selecting a task row selects the task AND offers its row commands.

3

Hierarchical date header

Two stacked date rows that follow the scale: Y = years · Q = years over quarters · M = years over months · W = months over week-start day numbers · D = months over day numbers (auto-thinned when tight). Separator ticks always follow the bottom row.

4

Material bars — move, resize, recolor, progress

Drag a bar's body to move it (vertically too — it changes row); drag its edges to resize; pick a swatch in the app bar to recolor. Label on the bar (Research/Design) or in the rail (Build's tasks); the global "Labels" button flips the whole chart to rail mode for a text-free graph.

5

Draggable markers shipped

Today (indigo) and deadline (red) lines drag horizontally with day snapping; labels editable; any number of custom markers.

6

Anchored notes

Text elements tether to a task or milestone (tick mark shows the link) and follow it when it moves; free-floating notes pin anywhere. Drag this one.

7

Dependencies as quiet elbows

Thin connectors, arrowhead at the target. Create one live: select a task → Link → click the target bar. They re-route when you move either end.

8

Bottom app bar is the command surface

PowerNote-style: contents swap with the selection (task / milestone / row / marker / note / nothing). Global controls (scale, labels, grid, insert) stay present. Right-click mirrors the same commands.
